CHAPTER 19

Inevitability

The fracture did not close.
It stabilized.

Reality accepted the damage.
And adjusted around it.

The Sovereigns felt the change.
Ferron did not rise at once.

Not from weakness.
From awareness.

What was taken would not return
as it once was.
Aetheria sensed it first.

Thought traveled farther now.
And found less resistance.

Something was closer.
Not present—closer.
The fracture responded.

Not to force.
Not to movement.

To attention.
To recognition.
No command was given.

No consensus formed.

Each Sovereign understood
in isolation—
inaction was no longer neutral.
They moved.

Not forward.
Not backward.

Away from certainty.
Into thinning law.
Power behaved incorrectly here.

Fire dimmed into absence.
Storm lost direction.

Life hurried toward conclusions
it had not yet earned.
They did not see Nihilus.

But the universe paused.

As if awaiting instruction.
Or permission.

Or inevitability.
