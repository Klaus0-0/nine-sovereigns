CHAPTER 17

The Seven Alone

The Nexus remained.
The divide remained.

What was gone—
was the feeling of togetherness.
Aelius stood beneath his sun.

Light still shone.
Heat still burned.

Yet leadership, without others to bear it,
felt hollow.
Glacius ruled a perfect stillness.

Ice did not crack.
Wind did not howl.

Stability, untouched by life,
revealed its own cruelty.
Thalor waited for the storm to answer.

It did not.

Power restrained by doubt
felt heavier than any chain.
Pyrrhus watched his fire burn smaller.

Not weaker.
Not dying.

Controlled—
and that frightened him more.
Verdani walked through uneven growth.

Some roots flourished.
Others withered unseen.

Balance no longer came naturally.
Aetheria saw futures overlapping.

Unity restored.
Unity destroyed.
Darkness stepping forward.

None were certain.
Morvus returned to shadow.

Here, silence did not judge.
Darkness did not ask questions.

He was not afraid to be alone.
Across distant realms,
a single realization took hold.

This separation was not temporary.

Something had changed forever.
Reality tightened—
not violently,
not visibly.

As if the universe itself
leaned closer to listen.
In the deepest shadow,
something stirred.

Not reaching.
Not calling.

Simply waiting.
They were seven sovereigns.

Separated by realms.
Bound by consequence.

And for the first time—
truly alone.
