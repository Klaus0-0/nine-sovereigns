CHAPTER 5

The Gathering

The disturbance did not announce itself.
It did not roar, or tear the sky apart.

It simply arrived —
everywhere, at once.
“This is not chaos born of excess.
I have felt that before.

This is something restrained…
straining to remain so.”
Space folded inward.
Not collapsing —
preparing.
“So it calls us again.

Not to celebrate order.
Not to mourn what was lost.

But because something we left behind
has begun to move.”
“Eight thrones stand.
Eight fragments endure.

We divided the whole to save the world…

Tell me —
which of you feels complete?”
“Do not mistake this silence for peace.

The realms are not wounded —
they are starving.

And starvation is born
not from absence alone…
but from denial.”
“Careful with your accusations.

We ended the chaos together.
We paid what was demanded of us.

If the world trembles now,
it is not because of what we took —
but because of what we refused to finish.”
“Unfinished?

We carved the wound ourselves.
Tore the whole into fragments
and called it balance.

And now you fear
what gathers in the cracks.”
“You overreach—”
“You always feared unity—”
“This throne was never meant for you—”

“Then why does it answer me?”
“Look at it.

Even now…
the center remains untouched.

Power gathers where order fails.

And something is listening —
not to our words…
but to our division.”
