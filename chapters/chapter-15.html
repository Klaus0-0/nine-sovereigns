CHAPTER 15

The Cost of Unity

The battlefield did not celebrate.
It did not mourn.

Broken stone lay where power had passed.
The air itself felt heavier—
as if it remembered what had been done.
They stood together again.

Not because they trusted one another.
Not because unity felt right.

But because separating now felt even more dangerous.
Pyrrhus steadied himself as flame faltered.

Fire answered slowly.
Weakly.

For the first time since his crowning, power did not rush to obey.
Thalor raised his hand to the sky.

The storm hesitated.
Clouds held still.

Control had limits— and he had just found them.
Verdani reached for life.

Growth began— then stopped.

Nature did not resist.
It simply… failed.
Aetheria saw what the others could not.

Paths repeating.
Fractures multiplying.

Unity again.
Damage again.

She said nothing.
Morvus stepped back into shadow.

Not in fear.
Not in defiance.

Calculation demanded distance.
Ferron watched them in silence.

Cracked armor.
Slowed breath.

The cost was visible— and he did not look away.
Voices rose—then fell.

Control.
Freedom.
Survival.

Every answer contradicted another.
Unity fractured from within.
No agreement was reached.

But a truth settled among them.

They could not face Ferron again— not like this.
Beyond the Nexus,
something pressed against reality.

Not demanding.
Not revealing.

Waiting.
Unity had saved them.

And in doing so— it had weakened them.

This was the price.
And it had only begun.
