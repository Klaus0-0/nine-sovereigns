CHAPTER 6
Pyrrhus and Thalor
The nexus did not call them by name.
It only allowed their wills to collide.

King Pyrrhus burned because stillness felt like death.
King Thalor waited because judgment never rushed.

Between them, silence fractured first.
“You mistake restraint for hesitation,” Thalor spoke calmly.
“Power does not prove itself by how loudly it announces intent.”

Lightning gathered — measured, deliberate.
Not unleashed.

Judgment does not hurry to be heard.
Pyrrhus answered without permission.
Flame surged forward, absolute and unrelenting.

The impact bent the chamber itself.
Stone screamed. Space tore apart.

This was not a challenge — it was refusal.
“I will not be contained,” Pyrrhus roared through scorched breath.
“If the world must burn to move forward—”

Flames erupted again, tearing through resistance.
Armor cracked. Flesh burned.

He advanced anyway.
Thalor did not answer with force.
He answered with precision.

Lightning sliced through flame mid-surge.
Momentum collapsed into nothing.

“And this,” he said quietly, “is why you fail.”
Far above the battlefield, someone watched.
Not with concern — but with hunger.

*Yes…*
*Break each other…*

*Show me what power costs.*
Control fractured.
Rage turned inward.

Power bled through broken armor and skin.
Lightning misfired. Flames choked.

Neither king could stop now.
Pyrrhus fell first — not defeated, but spent.
Thalor followed — not victorious, but emptied.

The nexus offered no judgment.
Only silence.

This was the price of excess.
Blood stained the stone floor.
Embers faded into ash.

Lightning twitched weakly inside the cracks.
The space remembered what happened here.

Power, once spilled, never returns unchanged.
