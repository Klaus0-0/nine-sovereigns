CHAPTER 30

The Break

It held.

Perfectly.

For a moment.
Then—

something shifted.

Not enough to see.
Enough to matter.
He remained still.

As if nothing had changed.

As if everything had.
The first fracture formed.

Small.

Irreversible.
Alignment failed.

Not entirely.

Enough.
One noticed.

Too late to prevent.

Early enough to understand.
The structure shifted.

Geometry betrayed itself.

Perfection lost.
Fractures spread.

Not violently.

Inevitably.
He moved.

Just once.

And everything followed.
They felt it break.

Not outside.

Within.
The construct collapsed.

Not destroyed.

Unmade.
Silence returned.

He stood free.

As before.
Nothing had changed.

Except them.
