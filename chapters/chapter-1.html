CHAPTER 1
The World Before Sovereigns
Before the Sovereigns were named,
before crowns were forged from will and power,
the world already carried scars.
Mountains cracked without war.
Oceans shifted without command.
The sky pressed low, heavy with something unfinished.
Power existed long before rulers did.
It flowed freely—
wild, unmeasured, unanswered.
Those who touched it were changed.
Those who chased it were broken.
In the beginning, there were no kings.
No queens.
No thrones.
Only beings who learned—too late—
that power does not ask who deserves it.
The first conflicts were not battles.
They were accidents.
A thought that burned too bright.
A hand that reached too far.
A will that refused to yield.
Land tore open.
Time bent.
Silence learned how to scream.
When the force finally faded,
nothing rushed to replace it.
The world did not heal.
It simply remained—
broken, quieter than before.
Chaos demanded an answer.
And the world did not wait for one to be given.
Someone stepped forward.
Not to save the world—
but to control it.
This was not the beginning of peace.
It was the beginning of order.
And every order demands a price.
