CHAPTER 10

Fire and Storm

The nexus did not demand violence.
It merely held them together long enough
for pride to speak before reason.

Fire stood unmoving.
Storm descended in silence.

Neither bowed.
Neither listened.
“You mistake endurance for authority,”
Thalor said, his voice carried by thunder.

“You burn because you fear what survives you.”

Lightning answered before Pyrrhus could.
The sky broke downward.
Judgment chose speed.
Pyrrhus did not retreat.
He never had.

“I burn because the world remembers me,”
he roared into the storm.

“I burn so nothing forgets what defiance costs.”

Fire rose to meet the sky.
When fire met storm,
the nexus screamed.

Space bent away from the impact.
Stone forgot its shape.

This was not combat.
It was collision.
And the world paid first.
Flames spread without asking permission.

Pyrrhus pushed forward,
each step carving molten scars
into a realm never meant to bleed.

“Kneel,” he demanded,
mistaking destruction for dominance.
Storm answered not with rage—
but precision.

Lightning cut through fire,
dividing it, shaping it,
turning fury against itself.

“Power,” Thalor said calmly,
“is not volume.”
Neither noticed the cost.

Fire burned too deep.
Storm struck too long.

The nexus fractured beneath them—
not from impact,
but from excess.

Victory became irrelevant.
When the light faded,
silence returned wounded.

The battlefield remained—
broken, hollowed, altered forever.

No crown was lifted.
No triumph declared.

Only damage endured.
✦ ✦ ✦
From beyond the fracture,
something watched.

Not with anger.
Not with urgency.

“They still believe this is about strength,”
Nihilus whispered to the void.

“Let them keep fighting.”
“They are teaching me everything.”
